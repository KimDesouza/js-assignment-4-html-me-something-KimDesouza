Visiting Las Vegas, Zion National Park and Death Valley

Earlier this year in March, I decided to go on a trip with my best friend for my birthday. One of the places I had wanted to visit was Las Vegas after hearing about how fun and lively a city it was. We wanted to also visited a few other places while we were there so our Vegas trip ended up being more of a mini road trip around Nevada, Utah and Arizona. We decide to go for a week and visited the Las Vegas Strip, Zion National Park and Death Valley.

Las Vegas, NV

We wanted to try everything that we could in Vegas that we couldn't experience here in the midwest. When we landed in Vegas, we had to try in & out which was the first place we went to after picking up our rental car. We then headed to our hotel, The Signature at MGM Grand on the Las Vegas Strip. The drive to the hotel down the strip was incredible. The whole street was lit up and we got to see so many incredible places like the Cosmopolitan and the Bellagio Hotel.

The next day we walked down the strip to the Cosmopolitan for brunch. We then went on the Big Bus tour which took us through some of the landmarks in Las Vegas. The first stop we got of at was for the High Roller/LINQ Promenade where they had a version of a version of the Ferris Wheel ride and a Zipline. The High Roller is the largest observation wheel in North America whith an amazing view of the City or Las Vegas from 550 feet high.The Fly LINQ zipline is 12 stories above the LINQ promenade (a street with various different restaurants and stores) with speeds of up to 35 miles per hour.

After the visit to the LINQ Promenade we went to The Strat Hotel. We went up to the SkyPod which was an observation deck at the top of the hotel which also had an incredible view of the city. They also had a few rides one of which was the X-Scream. The X-Scream is a giant teeter-totter ride on the edge of the building about 866 feet high. The ride propels you headfirst, 27 feet over the edge of the tower.

Once we were done at The Strat, we had dinner at a Thai restaurant and got to try some new dishes that were delicious. After dinner we headed back to the hotel at the end of the night. The next day we decided to go to the New York, New York Hotel to try the Big Apple Roller Coaster Ride which was incredibly thrilling.

Later that evening we went to watch the Cirque du Soleil - Mystere show at the Treasure Island Hotel. While waiting for the show we decided to try playing at the casino and ended up winning $100. We then went in for the show which had incredible performance. I would definitely recommend going to watch at least one if possible.

Zion National Park

The next day we drove to Zion National Park where we had booked a UTV tour on the Peak-A-Boo and Breat Chamber Trail. The tour included driving the utv and hiking to some of the views. Some of the hikes were though the canyons and up to the sand dunes which were absolutley beautiful. We even got to drive the utv a few time with out tour guide which was such a unique experience very much different to driving a car and even more thrilling. Overall, it was a very fun experience that I would definitley try again however, we were both very exhausted by the end of the day and decided to return to out hotel to rest.

On our last day we returned to Las Vegas and decided to go to Chinatown to try a few restaurants there. We had sushi at a revolving sushi bar which was an interesting first time experience. We also picked up some treats at a bakery such as custard buns, lemon cupcakes, mango cake and bubble tea. Before heading to the airport we decided to visit the Las Vegas Sign for some picture.

Overall, it was a wonderful trip with various different experiences. I would like to re-visit Vegas again as there are still so many things to do there that I didn't get the chance to. I would definitely recommend visiting when possible because it's a very memorable place with lots of things to do.
